<p><b>Prologis, Inc.</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Multinational_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Multinational company">multinational</a> logistics <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trust</a> (<a href="/wiki/REIT" class="mw-redirect" title="REIT">REIT</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-pr-home_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-home-3">[3]</a></sup> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>. The company was formed through the merger of AMB Property Corporation and ProLogis<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> in June 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> which made Prologis the largest industrial real estate company in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> Currently the world's largest owner of warehouses<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-baskin_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-baskin-6">[6]</a></sup> and distribution centers,<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-rents_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-rents-7">[7]</a></sup> Prologis operates as a <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded">publicly traded</a> company<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a><sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sandpfive_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sandpfive-9">[9]</a></sup> The company is the owner, manager and developer<sup id="cite_ref-pr-home_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-home-3">[3]</a></sup> of approximately 3,382<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Report_Quarterly_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Report_Quarterly-10">[10]</a></sup> logistics and <a href="/wiki/Distribution_facility" class="mw-redirect" title="Distribution facility">distribution center</a><sup id="cite_ref-forbes-kacsmar_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-kacsmar-11">[11]</a></sup> and logistics facilities<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> in markets across the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-prologisprofile_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologisprofile-13">[13]</a></sup> and serves approximately 5,000 customers<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Report_Quarterly_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Report_Quarterly-10">[10]</a></sup> across a diverse range of industries<sup id="cite_ref-forbes-kacsmar_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-kacsmar-11">[11]</a></sup> in 19 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-pr-home_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-home-3">[3]</a></sup> As of December 31, 2017, Prologis had $79 billion in total assets under management.<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Report_Quarterly_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Report_Quarterly-10">[10]</a></sup> The company's research department publishes <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">white papers</a> and its own <a href="/wiki/Market_research" title="Market research">market research</a>, including the quarterly <i>Industrial Business Indicator</i> and the annual <i>Prologis Logistics Rent Index</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PLRI_14-0" class="reference"><a </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1983-2011:_Background">1983-2011: Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1983-2011: Background" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Prologis was formed in 2011 through the merger of AMB and ProLogis, both multinational real estate companies based in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="AMB_Property_Corporation">AMB Property Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: AMB Property Corporation" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1983, <a href="/wiki/Hamid_R._Moghadam" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamid R. Moghadam">Hamid Moghadam</a> and Doug Abbey used a $50,000 line of credit to found Abbey, Moghadam and Company,<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> an investment manager serving institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> Although they initially planned to provide investment advisory services, according to <i>Forbes,</i> they soon became known for instead "helping investors revive underperforming assets."<sup id="cite_ref-forbes-kacsmar_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-kacsmar-11">[11]</a></sup> The company was joined by T. Robert Burke in 1984 and established AMB Property Corporation, a legacy Prologis company that invested in office, industrial and community <a href="/wiki/Shopping_centers" class="mw-redirect" title="Shopping centers">shopping centers</a> on behalf of large <a href="/wiki/Institutional_investor" title="Institutional investor">institutional investors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> During the collapse of the office building market in the late 1980s, the company avoided significant financial repercussions by investing in industrial parks and shopping centers beforehand,<sup id="cite_ref-forbes-kacsmar_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-kacsmar-11">[11]</a></sup> and beginning to exit the office market in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> AMB launched its first private equity fund in 1989, which focused on industrial and retail properties.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In late 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> AMB closed its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> (IPO) with more than US$2.8 billion under management.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> Selling its retail business around 1999 to focus solely on the industrial sector, the company made its first overseas investment in 2002, developing a facility for <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> That year AMB initiated an international expansion program<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> focused on buying and developing distribution facilities near global trade hubs, particularly in growth markets such as <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes-kacsmar_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-kacsmar-11">[11]</a></sup> AMB added an internal development division in 2004. By 2011 AMB was focusing on expanding its operations in China and Brazil, at which point the company announced it was considering a merger with ProLogis, formerly Security Capital Trust.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Security_Capital_Industrial_Trust">Security Capital Industrial Trust</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Security Capital Industrial Trust" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Security Capital Industrial Trust (SCI), a legacy company to Prologis, was <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_business" class="mw-redirect" title="Incorporated business">incorporated</a> in 1991. SCI made an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> (<a href="/wiki/IPO" class="mw-redirect" title="IPO">IPO</a>) on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> The company first expanded outside the United States in 1996 with acquisitions in Mexico<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> and went international in 1997<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> with Security Capital Industrial Trust moving into both Mexico and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. SCI moved into the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in the summer of 1997, and afterwards expanded into <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>. SCI purchased 33 percent of the <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> software company Insight Inc. in October 1997, to help solve supply-chain problems for clients. SCI also expanded into the refrigerated distribution business in 1997, purchasing <a href="/wiki/Christian_Salvesen" title="Christian Salvesen">Christian Salvesen</a> and changing it into CS Integrated LLC. SCI furthermore acquired Texas Cold Storage and Continental Freezer in 1997, and secured the primary contract to distribute food for <a href="/wiki/Kroger_Co." class="mw-redirect" title="Kroger Co.">Kroger Co.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>. For refrigeration abroad, SCI purchased <a href="/wiki/Posten_Norge" title="Posten Norge">Frigoscandia AB</a>, then "Europe's largest distributor of refrigerated goods."<sup id="cite_ref-biz-denver_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biz-denver-16">[16]</a></sup> SCI later exited the cold-storage business.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-cold_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-cold-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>After Prologis, Inc. was incorporated on November 24, 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> SCI officially changed its name to ProLogis in July 1998, at which point it had already gone by StarLogis abroad. SCI was active in 84 markets in 12 countries by July 1998, with a market capitalization of nearly $5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-biz-denver_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biz-denver-16">[16]</a></sup> In November 1998, Prologis Trust acquired Meridian Industrial Trust for $862.5 million in stock. At the time, Prologis was the "largest owner of industrial and warehouse properties in the United States."<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-meridian_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-meridian-18">[18]</a></sup> ProLogis formed its first property funds in 1999, and entered the Japanese market in 2001. In 2003, ProLogis joined the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> and entered the Chinese market,<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> and in 2004 the company acquired Keystone Industrial Trust for $1.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-nrei-keystone_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrei-keystone-19">[19]</a></sup> In the same year, ProLogis formed its first joint venture in China with Suzhou Logistics Center Co. Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> With headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Aurora,_Colorado" title="Aurora, Colorado">Aurora, Colorado</a>, at the time, in 2005 ProLogis completed a merger with <a href="/wiki/Catellus_Development_Corporation" title="Catellus Development Corporation">Catellus Development Corporation</a>, a North American industrial development company, for $4.9 billion. That year, ProLogis ranked #1 in the 2005 Top 25 Industrial Owners survey.<sup id="cite_ref-teig-nrei_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teig-nrei-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2006, ProLogis became a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_1000" title="Fortune 1000">Fortune 1000</a> company and the ProLogis European Properties Fund went public.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In the midst of problems with debt<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup> following aggressive expansion<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> and heavy borrowing,<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> ProLogis' CEO Jeffrey Schwartz was replaced with Walter Rakowich in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup> Rakovich afterwards implemented a cost-cutting effort involving raising capital and selling assets.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> In December 2008, ProLogis sold a portfolio of assets to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Singapore_Investment_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Singapore Investment Corporation">Government of Singapore Investment Corporation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> and it sold its China operations and some of its Japanese interests to <a href="/wiki/GIC_Private_Limited" title="GIC Private Limited">GIC Real Estate</a> for $1.3 billion in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-future_15-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-15">[15]</a></sup> That August, ProLogis secured financing from <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Pfandbriefbank" title="Deutsche Pfandbriefbank">Deutsche Pfandbriefbank</a> and an unidentified German bank for two of its funds.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-warehouse_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-warehouse-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Blackstone_Group_LP" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackstone Group LP">Blackstone Group LP</a> bought a portfolio of ProLogis assets in late 2010 for $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> After acquiring Catellus Development Corp. in 2005 for $3.6 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup>  in January 2011 ProLogis sold a portfolio of Catellus retail and mixed-use assets to affiliates of <a href="/wiki/TPG_Capital" title="TPG Capital">TPG Capital</a> for $505 million, including rights to the Catellus name.<sup id="cite_ref-finkelstein_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-finkelstein-22">[22]</a></sup> Prior to its final merging with ProLogis in 2011, AMB Property Corp AMB.N formed a €470 million joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Allianz" title="Allianz">Allianz</a> Real Estate in March 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters-amb_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-amb-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Formation_of_Prologis">2011: Formation of Prologis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011: Formation of Prologis" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011, AMB Property Corporation agreed to buy the larger ProLogis for $8.7 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-reuters-amb_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-amb-23">[23]</a></sup> with the new entity to be named Prologis (NYSE:PLD)<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyt-amb_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-amb-24">[24]</a></sup> and remain on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-chu_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-chu-25">[25]</a></sup> The companies announced that the new Prologis would be based in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, AMB's hometown, but would maintain an office in <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" class="mw-redirect" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>, where ProLogis was based.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> Completed in June 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> the “merger of equals”<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyt-amb_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-amb-24">[24]</a></sup> was described as "one of the biggest real-estate deals since the [2008] recession,"<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> and it created the largest industrial real estate company in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> With total market value estimated at $24 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-amb_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-amb-24">[24]</a></sup>  the new Prologis had around $46 billion in assets under management<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> and logistics and distribution facilities<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> in North America, Europe, Asia and South America.<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup> Clients of the new company included <a href="/wiki/DHL" title="DHL">DHL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuehne_%2B_Nagel" title="Kuehne + Nagel">Kuehne &amp; Nagel International AG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_Depot_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Home Depot Inc.">Home Depot Inc.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/FedEx" title="FedEx">FedEx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> AMB CEO <a href="/wiki/Hamid_R._Moghadam" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamid R. Moghadam">Hamid Moghadam</a> and ProLogis CEO Walter Rakowich were appointed as the new company's co-chiefs, with Moghadam to become the sole CEO in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-giants_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-giants-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-2014:_International_ventures">2013-2014: International ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013-2014: International ventures" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Prologis was among the largest 200 companies in the United States by June 2011, with a market cap of $15 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-forbesbiggest_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbesbiggest-12">[12]</a></sup> activity in 22 countries and 22 funds. By the fourth quarter of 2011, Prologis had sold a variety of assets and raised $1 billion, with plans to raise a further $2 billion through asset sales. Occupancy at Prologis real estate at the end of 2011 was 92 percent. In 2012, the company announced it planned to spend around $1 billion to develop projects in the United States, Brazil, China and Japan. By March 2012, Prologis was "the sixth-largest U.S. <a href="/wiki/REIT" class="mw-redirect" title="REIT">REIT</a> in terms of equity market capitalization, about $15 billion," and by May, Prologis had assets valued at around $10 billion in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-robaton_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-robaton-5">[5]</a></sup> On February 14, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-chu_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-chu-25">[25]</a></sup> Nippon Prologis REIT, Inc. (NPR),<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> a Japanese real estate investment trust formed by Prologis, successfully completed an IPO on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nippon-ipo_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nippon-ipo-26">[26]</a></sup> In March 2013, Prologis formed the Prologis European Logistics Partners Sarl joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Norges_Bank_Investment_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Norges Bank Investment Management">Norges Bank Investment Management</a> for USD$3.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-close-euro_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-close-euro-27">[27]</a></sup>  Prologis had $46.9 billion of assets under management as of September 2013. By November 2013, Prologis, Inc. remained the world's biggest warehouse owner, with plans to spend "as much as $600 million a year to develop warehouses in Japan," its biggest market after the United States. At the time, Prologis had customers such as <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Express" title="Nippon Express">Nippon Express</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-chu_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-chu-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>By the summer of 2014, Prologis was active in 21 countries, with around 1,400 real estate professionals.<sup id="cite_ref-prologisprofile_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologisprofile-13">[13]</a></sup>  The company also had around $51 billion in total assets under 
management,<sup id="cite_ref-prologisprofile_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologisprofile-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10Q_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10Q-28">[28]</a></sup> with $27.3 billion of that amount in investment management.<sup id="cite_ref-prologisprofile_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologisprofile-13">[13]</a></sup> Prologis completed its IPO for FIBRA Prologis in June 2014, the company’s Mexican affiliate also known as Prologis Property Mexico SA, raising about 7 billion pesos ($541 million).<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-raises1_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-raises1-29">[29]</a></sup> At the end of 2014, Prologis owned 2,853 properties, with the bulk in North America and around 600 in Europe and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-tobuy_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-tobuy-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2015-2016:_Recent_sales">2015-2016: Recent sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015-2016: Recent sales" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Prologis had reduced its number of managed funds from 23 to 11 by the spring of 2015, and was managing "$29 billion in real-estate assets through joint ventures and a series of funds backed by institutional investors."<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-tobuy_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-tobuy-30">[30]</a></sup> That financial quarter, earnings were boosted by the sale of a <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> industrial park to <a href="/wiki/Facebook,_Inc." title="Facebook, Inc.">Facebook, Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-wsj-rents_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-rents-7">[7]</a></sup> With corporate headquarters remaining in San Francisco, in April 2015 Prologis announced a plan to move offices from east <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" class="mw-redirect" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>, to a new building in downtown Denver<sup id="cite_ref-denver-up_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-denver-up-31">[31]</a></sup> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-current-hq_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-current-hq-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>Also that month, Prologis partnered with <a href="/wiki/Norges_Bank_Investment_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Norges Bank Investment Management">Norges Bank Investment Management</a> to buy KTR Capital Partners for $5.9 billion, expanding Prologis' real estate portfolio in the United States<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-tobuy_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-tobuy-30">[30]</a></sup> through the Prologis U.S. Logistics Venture.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-raises_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-raises-33">[33]</a></sup> At the time, Prologis had a market value of around $22 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-tobuy_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-tobuy-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-raises_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-raises-33">[33]</a></sup> In October 2015, Prologis acquired Morris Realty Associates' portfolio of logistics and retail properties in the United States for $820 million. Prologis sold the retail asset portion of this portfolio to an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a> for $374 million.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>By late 2016, Prologis had built large multistory warehouses in Japan, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and China.<sup id="cite_ref-bayview_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayview-35">[35]</a></sup> The company began construction on a three-floor warehouse in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, in November 2016, which was the first multi-story warehouse in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-buildfirst_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-buildfirst-36">[36]</a></sup>  The following month, Prologis also outlined plans to build a three-story warehouse in San Francisco.<sup id="cite_ref-bayview_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayview-35">[35]</a></sup> Prologis received two 2016 Eurobuild CEE Awards in December 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-budapest_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-budapest-37">[37]</a></sup> at which point<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-buildfirst_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-buildfirst-36">[36]</a></sup> Prologis Inc. remained the world's largest owner of warehouses<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-baskin_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-baskin-6">[6]</a></sup> and distribution centers,<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-rents_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-rents-7">[7]</a></sup> with a 97.1% occupancy rate.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-top_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-top-38">[38]</a></sup> As of December 31, 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Report_Quarterly_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Report_Quarterly-10">[10]</a></sup> Prologis Inc. was one of the world's largest industrial property owners.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-keikom_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-keikom-39">[39]</a></sup> As of December 31, 2017, Prologis had $78.7 billion in total assets under management, with 2,282 industrial properties<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Report_Quarterly_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Report_Quarterly-10">[10]</a></sup> in 19 countries. It also had 1,600 employees<sup id="cite_ref-pr-home_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-home-3">[3]</a></sup> and continued to be traded on the NYSE.<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_model">Business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Business model" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded">publicly traded</a> company<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> and member of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-sandpfive_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sandpfive-9">[9]</a></sup> Prologis operates in the Americas, Europe, and Asia with a portfolio of real-estate ownings or joint-ventures<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> in 19 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-pr-home_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-home-3">[3]</a></sup> Operating segments distinguish real estate operations from strategic capital, with distribution facilities leased to customers<sup id="cite_ref-googlefinance_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googlefinance-8">[8]</a></sup> in both the <a href="/wiki/Business-to-business" title="Business-to-business">business-to-business</a> and retail/online fulfillment categories.<sup id="cite_ref-pp-now_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pp-now-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>Prologis focuses on three key lines of business:
</p>
<ul><li>Real estate operations (leasing, managing buildings)<sup id="cite_ref-prologisprofile_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologisprofile-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prologis-www_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologis-www-41">[41]</a></sup></li>
<li>Capital deployment (development and acquisition of properties)<sup id="cite_ref-prologis-cd_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologis-cd-42">[42]</a></sup></li>
<li>Strategic capital (raising capital and co-investing in portfolios)<sup id="cite_ref-10Q_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10Q-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prologis-www_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prologis-www-41">[41]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Market_research">Market research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prologis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Market research" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Through its in-house research department, the company publishes proprietary market research and several <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">white papers</a> each year. These include the <i>Industrial Business Indicator</i>, published on a quarterly basis, and the <i>Prologis Logistics Rent Index</i>, published annually<sup id="cite_ref-PLRI_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PLRI-14">[14]</a></sup> since 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-index_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-index-43">[43]</a></sup> In 2017, the company partnered with Oxford Economics on in-house research, determining that $1.3 trillion-worth of goods were transferred through its buildings annually. The research also asserted that clusters of logistics assets were economically beneficial to their local areas, with estimations that Prologis and its customers had a total GDP economic impact of $200 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Economic_Impact_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Economic_Impact-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
