<p><b>Arconic</b> is a company specializing in lightweight metals engineering and manufacturing. Arconic's products, which include aluminum, titanium, and nickel, are used worldwide in aerospace, automotive, commercial transportation, packaging, building and construction,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> oil and gas, defense, consumer electronics, and industrial applications. Arconic in known as the supplier of the <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Tower_fire" title="Grenfell Tower fire">Grenfell Tower cladding</a>.

href="#cite_note-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Alcoa Inc. was renamed Arconic Inc., and retained the operations in aluminum rolling (excluding the Warrick operations), aluminum plate, precision castings, and aerospace and industrial fasteners.<sup id="cite_ref-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Arconic_sells_60_percent_stake_in_Alcoa_for_$890_million_11-1" class="reference"><a class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_13-0" class="reference"><a and other lightweight metals into engineered products such as <a href="/wiki/Turbine" title="Turbine">turbine</a> blades for sectors including aerospace and automotive.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_15-0" Elliott publicly called for the firing of then CEO, <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Kleinfeld" title="Klaus Kleinfeld">Klaus Kleinfeld</a> citing the company's lackluster stock performance, missed profit forecasts and inefficient spending.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal,_April_18_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal,_April_18-21">[21]</a></sup> On April 17, 2017, Klaus Kleinfeld resigned as chairman and CEO by mutual agreement with the board of Arconic, after sending an unauthorized letter to Elliott.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Grenfell_Tower">Grenfell Tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arconic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Grenfell Tower" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>For the £11 million renovation of <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Tower" title="Grenfell Tower">Grenfell Tower</a>, Arconic provided one component of the <a href="/wiki/Cladding_(construction)" title="Cladding (construction)">cladding</a>, known as Reynobond PE <a href="/wiki/Aluminum_composite_panels" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminum composite panels">aluminum composite panels</a>, to Omnis Exteriors. Reynobond PE was not the most <a href="/wiki/Fire_retardant" title="Fire retardant">fire-retardant</a> option.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 26 June 2017, short time after the <a href="/wiki/Grenfell_Tower_fire" title="Grenfell Tower fire">Grenfell Tower fire</a> which resulted in the death of 72 people, Arconic issued a statement that it would no longer sell its Reynobond PE (polyethylene - aluminum composite cladding) for use in high-rise buildings. The company said this is applicable worldwide due to the difficulty of being sure that its material would be used in a way compliant with building regulations in various countries.<sup id="cite_ref-davies_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davies-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcgren_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcgren-25">[25]</a></sup> An inquiry into the fire in 2018 found that the cladding, which incorporated polyethylene material within, was the primary cause of the spread of the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The fire resulted in several lawsuits against Arconic.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
