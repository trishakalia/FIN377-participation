</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandria_Real_Estate_Equities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Most of Alexandria's properties are offices and laboratories, and it focuses on renting to life science and technology companies.  As of December 31, 2018, Alexandria had 22 million square feet of operating space located in more than 230 properties.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-1">[1]</a></sup>  Tenants include <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eli_Lilly_and_Company" title="Eli Lilly and Company">Eli Lilly</a> and <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">GlaxoSmithKline</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>  It has properties in various regions throughout the United States, including Greater Boston, San Francisco, New York City, San Diego, Seattle, Maryland, and <a href="/wiki/Research_Triangle" title="Research Triangle">Research Triangle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>  As of December 31, 2018, 37% of rental revenue was from properties in the Greater Boston Area, 23% was from properties in the San Francisco area, and 16% was from properties in the San Diego area.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-1">[1]</a></sup>  Alexandria tries to locate its properties around universities, as opposed to more distant suburban locations.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Alexandria also has a <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> arm, Alexandria Venture Investments, which primarily invests in life sciences firms.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandria_Real_Estate_Equities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>PHW was founded in January 2014 by Pramod Kumar, Poonam Kumari.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">[6]</a></sup>  It was named after <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria, Egypt</a>, because of that city's connection to science.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>  It originally began as a collaboration between Marcus and <a href="/wiki/Jacobs_Engineering_Group" title="Jacobs Engineering Group">Jacobs Engineering</a> to provide laboratories and office space to biotech firms.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup>  Its first purchase was of four buildings in San Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup>  It completed an IPO in 1997, raising $155 million.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup>  In 2017, it joined the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandria_Real_Estate_Equities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Properties" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Alexandria has a cluster of properties in <a href="/wiki/Kendall_Square" title="Kendall Square">Kendall Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, and is a major landlord in that neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">[8]</a></sup>  Its original investment in the area was in 2002, and it has invested at least $2.3 billion in properties in the neighborhood since then.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">[8]</a></sup>  Its largest campus is the Alexandria Center at Kendall Square, and other campuses include <a href="/wiki/Technology_Square_(Cambridge,_Massachusetts)" title="Technology Square (Cambridge, Massachusetts)">Alexandria Technology Square</a> and the Alexandria Center at One Kendall Square.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The Alexandria Center for Life Science in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> has a number of tenants in the biotech industry.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Alexandria has a number of properties in the <a href="/wiki/University_City,_San_Diego" title="University City, San Diego">University City</a> neighborhood of San Diego.
</p><p>Alexandria was the developer of the West Tower of the <a href="/wiki/MaRS_Discovery_District" title="MaRS Discovery District">MaRS Discovery District</a> in Toronto, which began construction in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  The company stopped construction in 2008, due to the economic downturn, and was bought out by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ontario" title="Government of Ontario">Ontario government</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandria_Real_Estate_Equities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
