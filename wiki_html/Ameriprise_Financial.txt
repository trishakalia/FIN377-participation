</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Timeline" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Ameriprise Financial began life as Investors Syndicate in 1894. Here are a few of the company's key milestones:
</p>
<ul><li>1894 – John Tappan founds Investors Syndicate in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>.</li>
<li>1925 – <a href="/wiki/United_States_West_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="United States West Coast">West Coast</a> businessman J. R. Ridgway. merges his investment firm with Investors Syndicate, takes over as president.</li>
<li>1937 – Upon the death of then 50-year-old J. R. Ridgway from <a href="/wiki/Leukemia" title="Leukemia">leukemia</a>, 23-year-old J. R. Ridgway, Sr. is appointed president.</li>
<li>1940 – Investors Syndicate introduces one of the first <a href="/wiki/Mutual_fund" title="Mutual fund">mutual funds</a>, the Investors Mutual Fund, giving clients new investing options and two advantages: diversification and professional management. By the 1960s, Investors Mutual Fund is the largest balanced mutual fund in the world.</li>
<li>1949 – Investors Syndicate changes its name to Investors Diversified Services, Inc. (IDS).</li>
<li>1958 – IDS founds the Investors Syndicate Life Insurance and Annuity Company (now known as <i><a href="/wiki/RiverSource" title="RiverSource">RiverSource</a> Life Insurance Company</i>).</li>
<li>1974 – the IDS Centre (now <a href="/wiki/IDS_Center" title="IDS Center">IDS Center</a>) is opened in downtown Minneapolis as the company's headquarters.</li>
<li>1979 – Ridgway family sells off last of its ownership interest. IDS became a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Alleghany_Corporation" title="Alleghany Corporation">Alleghany Corporation</a> pursuant to a <a href="/wiki/Merger" class="mw-redirect" title="Merger">merger</a>.</li>
<li>1984 – <a href="/wiki/American_Express" title="American Express">American Express</a> completes acquisition of IDS Financial Services from Alleghany Corporation.</li>
<li>1986 – IDS acquires Wisconsin Employers Casualty Company of <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay,_Wisconsin" title="Green Bay, Wisconsin">Green Bay</a> and renames it IDS Property Casualty Insurance Company.</li>
<li>1994 – IDS reaches $100 billion in assets and conducts business under the American Express brand.</li>
<li>1995 – IDS changes its name to American Express Financial Corporation, doing business as American Express Financial Advisors (AEFA).</li>
<li>2003 – AEFA acquires London-based <a href="/wiki/Threadneedle_Asset_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Threadneedle Asset Management">Threadneedle Asset Management</a> Holdings.</li>
<li>2005 – American Express spins off AEFA as an independent company named Ameriprise Financial, Inc., which begins trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Ticker_symbol" title="Ticker symbol">ticker symbol</a> AMP.</li>
<li>2006 – Ameriprise Financial launches Ameriprise Bank, FSB.</li>
<li>2008 – Ameriprise Financial acquires <a href="/wiki/H%26R_Block" title="H&amp;R Block">H&amp;R Block</a> Financial Advisors for $315 million, and the asset management firm <a href="/wiki/J._%26_W._Seligman_%26_Co." title="J. &amp; W. Seligman &amp; Co.">J. &amp; W. Seligman &amp; Co.</a> for $400 Million; declines U.S. Government financial assistance offered to many major American financial firms during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">financial crisis</a> occurring at the time.</li>
<li>2009 – Ameriprise Financial acquires long-term asset management business of <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbia Management">Columbia Management</a> for $1.2 billion from <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>.</li>
<li>2011 – Ameriprise Financial opens services to individuals in India with US$40,000 equivalent minimum income.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off_from_American_Express">Spin-off from American Express</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spin-off from American Express" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Ameriprise Financial is the successor to <i>American Express Financial Advisors</i> (AEFA), a former subsidiary of the <a href="/wiki/American_Express_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="American Express Company">American Express Company</a>. In 2005, American Express spun off AEFA as an independent company.  AEFA's new name, <i>Ameriprise Financial, Inc.</i> came into effect August 1, 2005, and the spin-off transaction closed on September 30, 2005.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_segments">Business segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Business segments" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Ameriprise Financial is one of the largest financial planning companies in the United States<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and is among the 25 largest asset managers in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-2010AnnualReport_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010AnnualReport-6">[6]</a></sup> It is ranked 246 in the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Ameriprise Financial is in <a href="/wiki/Wealth_management" title="Wealth management">wealth management</a> and retirement planning in the U.S. and also works as an asset manager with global business.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wealth_Management_and_Retirement">Wealth Management and Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wealth Management and Retirement" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Ameriprise Financial specializes in <a href="/wiki/Retirement_plans_in_the_United_States" title="Retirement plans in the United States">retirement</a>-related financial planning for relatively well-off clients. As of December 31, 2010, the company has about three-million clients and $672.7 billion in owned, managed and administered assets worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-2010AnnualReport_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010AnnualReport-6">[6]</a></sup> The company offers variable annuities and life and disability insurance. It also operates Ameriprise Bank, FSB which offers a variety of consumer banking and lending products and personal trust and related services.
</p><p>Ameriprise Financial received publicity for moving more quickly than some other full-service wealth management firms to develop <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> for its advisors and investors, e.g. via <a href="/wiki/LinkedIn" title="LinkedIn">LinkedIn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Asset_Management">Asset Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asset Management" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Since 2015 Ameriprise Financial's asset management arm operates under the name Columbia Threadneedle Investments. The company reported as having $436 billion in <a href="/wiki/Assets_under_management" title="Assets under management">assets under management</a> as of December 31, 2011, placing it eighth in long-term mutual fund assets in the U.S., fourth in retail funds in the U.K. and 27th in global assets under management.<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of Columbia Threadneedle is William F. Truscott.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal_brands">Principal brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Principal brands" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company uses three principal brands for its businesses in the United States: <i>Ameriprise Financial</i>, <i>Columbia Management</i> and <i>RiverSource</i>. The <i>Threadneedle</i> brand is used for its international asset manager's products.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Ameriprise_Financial_Services">Ameriprise Financial Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ameriprise Financial Services" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Ameriprise Financial Services, Inc., a financial planning and retail distribution subsidiary which operates under the Ameriprise Financial brand name, is a registered broker-dealer and registered investment adviser. It has an integrated model of comprehensive and personalized financial planning, diversified product manufacturing and affiliated and unaffiliated distribution through its network of financial advisors and registered representatives.<sup id="cite_ref-2010AnnualReport_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010AnnualReport-6">[6]</a></sup> The retail products and services that use the Ameriprise Financial brand include those that the company provides through its affiliated advisors (e.g., financial planning, investment advisory accounts, retail brokerage services and banking products) and products and services that it markets directly to consumers (e.g., personal auto and home insurance).<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Columbia_Management">Columbia Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Columbia Management" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>This was the primary brand for the company's U.S. asset management products and services, including retail and institutional asset management products. Following the completion of the acquisition of the long-term asset management business of the Columbia Management Group from Bank of America in April 2010, the company combined RiverSource Investments, its legacy U.S. asset management business, with Columbia Management, under the Columbia Management brand.<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup> It then merged the brand with Threadneedle Asset Management to create Columbia Threadneedle Investments in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-citywire_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-citywire-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="RiverSource">RiverSource</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: RiverSource" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>This is the brand for the company's annuities products and for the protection products issued by the RiverSource Life companies, including life and disability income insurance products.<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_of_Securities_America">Sale of Securities America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sale of Securities America" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Securities America Financial Corporation's roots go back to Financial Dynamics, an insurance marketing organization founded in 1984 by Steve Wild that had between 10,000 and 12,000 independent insurance agents affiliated with the firm. Financial Dynamics created Securities America, an independent securities broker/dealer, to use its insurance sales force to offer investments. Securities America Advisors Inc., an SEC-registered Investment Advisory firm, was founded in 1993 to offer investment management, financial advice and financial planning through a national network of independent financial advisors. In 1998, Securities America was acquired by American Express. In October 2005, after Ameriprise Financial's spin-off from American Express, Securities America became a wholly owned subsidiary of Ameriprise Financial, but continued to operate independently as a <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>-based broker/dealer and serving independent financial advisers across the country. Securities America acquired Brecek &amp; Young Advisors, a California-based broker/dealer, and its subsidiary, Iron Point Capital Management, from Security Benefit Corp. of Topeka, Kansas. Securities America had received the inaugural <i>Practitioner Thought Leadership Award</i> from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Retirement_Income_Industry_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Retirement Income Industry Association (page does not exist)">Retirement Income Industry Association</a> in March 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and was ranked as the seventh largest independent broker-dealer based on total revenues in the U.S., according to a June 2011 <i>Financial Planning</i> Magazine survey.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On April 25, 2011, Ameriprise announced that it was trying to find an "appropriate buyer" for Securities America Financial Corporation and its subsidiaries. Ameriprise said that this move will independent allow Securities America advisors and registered representatives who conduct business without utilizing the Ameriprise brand “to focus on growth opportunities in the independent channel.”<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
In November 2011, Ameriprise completed the sale of Securities America to <a href="/wiki/Ladenburg_Thalmann_Financial_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Ladenburg Thalmann Financial Services">Ladenburg Thalmann Financial Services</a> for $150 million in cash and potential future payments. Ameriprise claimed that selling this independent broker-dealer allows Ameriprise to focus its efforts on servicing and developing its branded advisor network.<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="International_subsidiaries">International subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International subsidiaries" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Threadneedle_Asset_Management_Holdings">Threadneedle Asset Management Holdings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Threadneedle Asset Management Holdings" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Threadneedle Asset Management Holdings SARL is a <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>-based holding company for the Threadneedle group of companies and a wholly owned independent subsidiary of Ameriprise Financial which provides <a href="/wiki/Investment_management" title="Investment management">investment management</a> products and services to clients in the United Kingdom, Europe, the Middle East and the Asia-Pacific region. Acquired by Ameriprise Financial in 2003,  Threadneedle initially operated as an independent international asset manager, under its own brand name, management organization and operating, compliance and technology infrastructure. Since 2015 it has been merged with Columbia Management to create the combined Columbia Threadneedle Investments brand.<sup id="cite_ref-citywire_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-citywire-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Threadneedle acquired Invesco Perpetual's full service defined contribution pension business with total assets of £470m,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> followed by acquisition of Standard Chartered Bank's World Express Funds investment funds business in 2009, the latter providing Threadneedle with an established Luxembourg-based SICAV platform with over US$2.38 billion worth assets under management as at April 30, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In August 2011 it was announced that Threadneedle would acquire the investment assets of <a href="/wiki/LV%3D" class="mw-redirect" title="LV=">LV=</a> amounting to some £8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
At December 31, 2010, Threadneedle had $105.6 billion in managed assets worldwide, and was expanding distribution of its institutional products in Europe, Asia, U.S., Middle East and Australia.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Ameriprise_India">Ameriprise India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Ameriprise India" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In early 2012, Ameriprise announced that it had begun offering retail financial planning and distribution services in India through its subsidiary, <i>Ameriprise India Private Limited</i>. Ameriprise has also established an insurance brokerage entity in India that
is licensed to deal in insurance products by India's <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Regulatory_and_Development_Authority" title="Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority">Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority</a> (IRDA). Ameriprise India has established offices in Delhi, Gurgaon, Greater Noida, Mumbai &amp; Pune, with a plan to expand to other Indian metro areas in the
future.
As with its U.S. financial planning business, Ameriprise India<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> provides holistic financial planning services through its
trained advisor force. Fees are received for delivering financial plans; however, Ameriprise India does not currently sell
affiliated investment or insurance products, instead refers clients who elect to implement their financial plan to third-party product manufacturers to purchase recommended investment and/or insurance products.
As of May 2014, Ameriprise has shut down the nascent financial planning division in India, citing poor prospects for fee-based advisory services.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions_and_restructuring">Acquisitions and restructuring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Acquisitions and restructuring" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In September 2009, Ameriprise Financial acquired Columbia Management, the long-term asset management business of <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>, for U.S. $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2013, Ameriprise Financial completed the conversion of its federal savings bank subsidiary, Ameriprise Bank, FSB, to a limited powers national trust bank, which conversion included changing the name of this subsidiary to Ameriprise National Trust Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Locations" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<ul><li>Ameriprise Financial's headquarters is located at 707 Second Avenue So., 55 Ameriprise Financial Center, <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> 55474.</li>
<li>It maintains executive offices at 1 World Trade Center, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> 10007.</li>
<li>Its offices in <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street" title="Wall Street">Wall Street</a>, New York provides portfolio management, banking, marketing and various support functions.</li>
<li>Ameriprise Financial's employee and <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">franchised</a> financial advisor branch offices are located throughout the United States.</li>
<li>Columbia Management is based in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, and has investment management teams in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, Minneapolis, New York, <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park,_California" title="Menlo Park, California">Menlo Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>.</li>
<li>RiverSource Life has offices in Minneapolis, <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay,_Wisconsin" title="Green Bay, Wisconsin">Green Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>, Boston, Los Angeles, New York and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.</li>
<li>RiverSource Life Insurance Company of New York and American Centurion Life are located in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_York" title="Albany, New York">Albany, New York</a>.</li>
<li>Ameriprise Advisor Center has locations in Minneapolis and <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ameriprise_Auto_%26_Home_Insurance" title="Ameriprise Auto &amp; Home Insurance">Ameriprise Auto &amp; Home Insurance</a> is located in Green Bay, Las Vegas and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>.</li>
<li>Threadneedle is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> and maintains its primary investment operations in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.</li>
<li>Ameriprise Financial also has employees located in offices at <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Capital_Region_(India)" title="National Capital Region (India)">National Capital Region (WNS &amp; Infosys DLF Phase -3, Gurgaon)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Ameriprise_advisors">Ameriprise advisors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Ameriprise advisors" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>As of December 31, 2011, Ameriprise Financial reported as having more than 9,700 branded advisors, the fifth largest branded advisor force in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-2011AnnualReport_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011AnnualReport-9">[9]</a></sup> Ameriprise Financial advisors recommend actions and a broad range of products consisting of investment, annuity, insurance, banking and other financial products to clients based on an appropriate range and level of risk. The financial advisors charge clients for financial advice and selling products. 
There are four ways the financial advisors affiliate with Ameriprise Financial:
</p>
<ul><li>Approximately 60% of Ameriprise's financial advisors are <a href="/wiki/Independent_contractor" title="Independent contractor">independent contractor</a> <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">franchisees</a> — they are not employed by Ameriprise, but are licensed registered representatives of Ameriprise and do not receive a salary from the parent company.</li>
<li>About one quarter of financial advisors are employed by Ameriprise ("employee financial advisors").  Ameriprise Financial Services, Inc. has the largest number of these professionals among any retail advisory force.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  The majority of the employee advisors work in local offices like their franchisee counterparts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Over 120 of the employee advisors work within the corporate office in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> as part of the Ameriprise Advisor Center (AAC).  These Advisors are fully licensed Securities and Insurance professionals that have received additional training which allows them to assist clients exclusively by telephone.  They are required to hold the <a href="/wiki/General_Securities_Representative_Exam" class="mw-redirect" title="General Securities Representative Exam">Series 7</a> and <a href="/wiki/Series_63" class="mw-redirect" title="Series 63">Series 63</a> <a href="/wiki/Financial_Industry_Regulatory_Authority" title="Financial Industry Regulatory Authority">Financial Industry Regulatory Authority</a> (FINRA) licenses, along with insurance licenses in all states they serve, and are encouraged to acquire their Chartered Retirement Planning Counselor (CRPC) professional designation from the College of Financial Planning, and are reimbursed for the cost.</li>
<li>The company also has associate Financial Advisors, employed by the independent contractor franchisees.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li></ul><p>Many Ameriprise advisors are <a href="/wiki/Certified_Financial_Planner" title="Certified Financial Planner">Certified Financial Planners</a>, and the company actively encourages attaining this designation.  Many advisors also hold similar designations such as the <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Life_Underwriter" class="mw-redirect" title="Chartered Life Underwriter">CLU</a> or <a href="/wiki/Financial_planner#History_of_certifications_in_financial_planning_across_the_globe" title="Financial planner">ChFC</a>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_accolades">Awards and accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Awards and accolades" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>With $12.075 billion in revenue, the company was ranked 252 on the 2018 <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a></i> list, a list of top 500 largest U.S. corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The company debuted on the list in 2007 at #297, reporting $8.1 billion in revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(8)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_controversy">Criticism and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Criticism and controversy" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-8 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-8">
<ul><li>Securities America was fined $5.4 million in 2003 for letting a broker work under a false name in its Orlando office and allegedly make bogus investments.<sup id="cite_ref-USA_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2005, Ameriprise agreed to pay a $12.3 million to settle <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Securities_Dealers" class="mw-redirect" title="National Association of Securities Dealers">NASD</a> charges relating to favorable treatment allegedly given to some mutual funds in exchange for brokerage business.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li>In mid-2005, the State of <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> reached a $7.4 million settlement with American Express Financial Advisors, alleging the company had violated the law by rewarding their financial advisers for recommending underperforming in-house mutual funds to clients.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li>Also in 2005, Ameriprise Financial entered into a $15 million settlement with the SEC for charges of <a href="/wiki/Market_timing" title="Market timing">market timing</a>. The Minnesota Department of Commerce levied $2 million in fines for similar market timing violations. The <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Securities_Dealers" class="mw-redirect" title="National Association of Securities Dealers">National Association of Securities Dealers</a> fined Ameriprise an additional $12.3 million for unsuitable share sales.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PR_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR-28">[28]</a></sup>  Ameriprise, having become a separate company, had not revealed which funds were timed, or the names of the people involved and the exact nature of the disciplinary action taken. <a href="/wiki/Morningstar,_Inc." title="Morningstar, Inc.">Morningstar</a> temporarily reduced the stewardship grade for Ameriprise's funds, although it did not impact the fund's overall star ratings from that firm.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li></ul><ul><li>In 2006, the NASD threatened to suspend the company for failing to pay an <a href="/wiki/Arbitration" title="Arbitration">arbitration</a> award to a former broker.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>In September 2006, Securities America reached a $16.3 million settlement with a group of <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="ExxonMobil Corporation">ExxonMobil Corporation</a> retirees for failing to supervise an associated broker.<sup id="cite_ref-exxon_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-exxon-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li></ul><ul><li>On July 11, 2007, the NASD fined Securities America $375,000 for improperly sharing directed brokerage commissions from a mutual fund company with a former Securities America broker.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Another NASD arbitration panel awarded $9.3 million to three retired <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> pilots against Securities America and a formerly associated broker for allegedly mishandling their savings. Other airline pilots have arbitration claims pending.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
<li>On July 10, 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a> (SEC) announced an enforcement action against Ameriprise for receiving millions of dollars in undisclosed revenue sharing as a condition for selling certain <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trusts</a> (REITs) to its brokerage customers. Ameriprise agreed to pay $17.3 million to settle the SEC's charges, however the period in which SEC states the violations took place were prior to the spinoff from American Express.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
<li>On April 15, 2011, Securities America, Inc. (SAI) and its holding company, Securities America Financial Corporation, entered into settlement agreements related to the sale of <a href="/wiki/Private_placement" title="Private placement">private placement</a> securities issued by Medical Capital and Provident Royalties that resulted in a $118 million pre-tax charge in the first quarter of 2011. The charge is in addition to a $40 million pre-tax charge in the fourth quarter of 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></li>
<li>On July 12, 2009, an advisor filed a complaint under 18 USC 1514(a) the whistleblower provisions of the Sarbanes Oxley Act.  The case was filed with OSHA.  OSHA determined the advisor was an independent contractor and not an employee.  The advisor was referred to FINRA Arbitration.  The Finra arbitration panel dismissed the case without a hearing- 4 years later.  All of the advisors clients were transferred to other advisors and his practice was destroyed.  He had no recourse to sell the equity in his practice in spite of a franchise agreement.  The case is set to go before a Federal Appeals judge next month.  See: DOL-ALJ website, case lookup, Ameriprise Financial.  2015 case dismissed.  2016 case now set for  hearings.</li>
<li>Ameriprise has settled other whistleblower complaints rather than have the facts revealed in public hearings.  See:  Loscalso v. Ameriprise.</li>
<li>Ameriprise also has a class action lawsuit and an SEC investigation into the use of WRAP accounts.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(9)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameriprise_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: See also" data-section="20" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-minerva-edit-enabled edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-9 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-9">
